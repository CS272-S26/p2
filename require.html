UW-Madison Computer Science Requirements

The Computer Science major at UW-Madison includes foundational computer science courses, mathematics requirements, and advanced computer science courses. This page gives a simplified overview of the major requirements to help students understand how the degree is structured. (Only courses relavent to major are considered)

Overview

  • Complete introductory computer science courses
  • Complete required mathematics courses
  • Complete advanced computer science coursework
  • Meet overall school and degree requirements

Introductory Computer Science Courses

These courses build the main foundation of the major and are required before moving into many upper-level CS classes.

  • Introduction to Discrete Mathematics – CS 240
  • Introduction to Computer Engineering – CS 252
  • Programming II – CS 300
  • Machine Organization and Programming – CS 354
  • Programming III – CS 400

Mathematics Requirements

Computer Science students also complete math courses that support problem solving, logic, and technical computing.

  • Basic Calculus – (MATH 221 & MATH 222) or (MATH 171 & MATH 217 & MATH 222)
  • Linear Algebra (1 course) – MATH 320 or MATH 340 or MATH 345 or MATH 341 or MATH 375
  • Probability or Statistics (1 course) – STAT/MATH 509 or STAT 311 or STAT 324 or MATH 331 or STAT 333 or STAT 340 or STAT 371 or STAT/MATH 341 or MATH 531

Advanced Computer Science Coursework

After completing core requirements, students choose from a variety of advanced CS courses. These courses allow students to explore different areas of computer science, such as Theory, Software & Hardware, Applications, Electives.

  • Theory of Computer Science (1 course)
  • Software & Hardware (2 courses)
  • Applications (1 course)
  • Electives (2 courses)

Additional Notes

Students must make sure they meet university, college, and degree requirements in addition to major requirements. The official UW-Madison guide should always be checked for the most current and complete requirement details.
