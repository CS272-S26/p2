UW-Madison Computer Science Requirements

The Computer Science major at UW-Madison includes foundational computer science courses, mathematics requirements, and advanced electives. This page gives a simplified overview of the major requirements to help students understand how the degree is structured.

Overview

  • Complete introductory and core computer science courses
  • Complete required mathematics courses
  • Complete advanced computer science coursework
  • Meet overall school and degree requirements

Core Computer Science Courses

These courses build the main foundation of the major and are usually completed before moving into many upper-level CS classes.

  • CS 300 – Programming II
  • CS 400 – Programming III
  • CS 354 – Machine Organization and Programming
  • CS 577 – Introduction to Algorithms

Mathematics Requirements

Computer Science students also complete math courses that support problem solving, logic, and technical computing.

  • Math 221 – Calculus and Analytic Geometry 1
  • Math 222 – Calculus and Analytic Geometry 2
  • Math 240 – Introduction to Discrete Mathematics
  • Math 340 or Math 341 – Linear Algebra

Advanced Computer Science Coursework

After completing core requirements, students choose from a variety of advanced CS courses. These courses allow students to explore different areas of computer science, such as artificial intelligence, systems, graphics, theory, and data science.

  • Advanced courses come from upper-level CS offerings
  • Students can use these courses to explore interests and possible career paths
  • Some advanced courses may require specific prerequisites

Additional Notes

Students must also make sure they meet university, college, and degree requirements in addition to major requirements. The official UW-Madison guide should always be checked for the most current and complete requirement details.
